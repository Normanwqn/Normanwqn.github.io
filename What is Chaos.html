What is Chaos?

Chaos by definition means "complete disorder and confusion". Or it is understood as the unpredictability of future events according to Edward Lorenz, who laid the foundation for the study of chaos in his 1963 paper "Deterministic Nonperiodic Flow".

Chaos: When the present determines the future, but the approximate present does not approximately determine the future.

Edward Lorenz

Lorenz's comment somehow shows that "chaos",which is a change in the future, is related to the current state.

Hence, the following will attempt to give a general overview on how chaos lurks across various unexpected fields which relies heavily on implementing differential equations

Pendulum? Simple Harmonic Oscillator?

In Physics, we prefer to overlook certain details in the certain relationship between different variables. We often use differential equations to describe the such relationship. Differential equations focus on how the change of one variable would correspond to the value of the differentials of the other variable and itself. For instance, in a pendulum, it is theorised that $${{{d^2}\theta } \over {d{t^2}}} + {g \over l}\sin \theta = 0$$ (the magnitude of angular acceleration is proportional to the sine of the angle).

Many of us are being told to simplify the term sine of θ as simply θ itself. Thus, the equation becomes $${{{d^2}\theta } \over {d{t^2}}} + {g \over l}\theta = 0$$

However, there is an apparent difference in the two models due to the simplification. The difference can be notably illustrated in using a phase space of the two pendulum (one only using θ and the other using sine of θ). A phase space usually illustrates variants of positions and momentum of a system. It is not a "real" space but its existence still helps us to grasp and study the state of a particular system. The concept of phase space will turn out to be extremely useful in Chaos Theory.

Nevertheless, all of these phases and models are trying to solve one fundamental problem: how to describe a Dynamical System. In reality, even though we have various differential equations, there is always something that is being overlooked whether it is friction, air drag, heat created by the swinging rope, etc. Many of these factors are chaotic.

Using differential equations is only one step to approximate the reality. However, the concept of approximation is fundamentally "flawed" as the element of non-linearity.
